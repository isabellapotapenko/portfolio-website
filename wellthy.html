Case Study: Wellthy

Wellthy: Health, Meet Wellness

Wellthy is a comprehensive wellness tracking product that will allow users to manage their medical records, health conditions, nutrition, and exercise routines all in one place.

The app also offers a social component where users can share photos, progress, and accomplishments.

The app aims to intersect medical health with wellness goals by providing users with personalized advice, information, and training based on their existing and pre-existing conditions.

STRATEGY

It all starts with understanding the problem.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Users of Wellthy need a way to access personalized health and wellness advice on their mobile devices because many of the health apps currently available do not recommend content relevant to their specific individual needs. We will know this to be true when we see users actively and continuously engaging with the suggested training and wellness materials and recommendations that correspond to their needs.

RESEARCH

What do the USERS want from a health and wellness app?

When conducing User Interviews, three research goals were kept in mind:

  1. To Understand what motivates users to use a health tracking app
  2. Determine why users stop using health tracking app
  3. What features users expect from a health tracking app

ANALAYSIS

Okay, time to give our target audience some personas!

PERSONA: FRANKIE

Frankie represents users that are more nutrition and health-focused-- they are less interested in fitness but want access to information that empowers them to be healthier! Frankie wants a health tracking app that keeps their health records in order and offers resources that will keep them in control of their health.

PERSONA: LILY

Lily represents users who have concrete fitness goals in mind! Users like Lily want personalized health and wellness advice to keep them motivated and help them reach their goals. Lily wants a health and wellness app that challenges them to be their healthiest version!

IDEATION

Time to explore all of the possible solutions.

With the target audience’s goals, needs, and motivations in mind, three key features were developed for Wellthy:

KEY FEATURES:

  1. Sync Health Records

    Once a user syncs their health records with Wellthy, the app can offer personalized health and wellness advice and activities specific to the user. Users can sync their health systems or manually upload a photo of their lab results.

  2. Select Four Week Exercise Program

    Wellthy will offer Exercise Programs of varying duration. Users can filter their preferred program duration and browse the selections without distractions!

  3. Meal Selection

    With users consent, Wellthy will offer meals based on users lab results, conditions, allergies, and preferences. User’s will be able to browse Meals that Wellthy selects just for them!

TEST

Lets put it in the hands of our target audience!

TEST OBJECTIVES

Sync: Observe whether participants can navigate to their ‘My Health’ page and successfully sync Wellthy with their clinics, networks, or hospital’s health system.

Exercise:Observe whether participants can select and complete the first task of a 4- Week Exercise Program by navigating to the “Exercise Programs” home page, setting the “Duration” filter to 4-Weeks, and selecting the first generated program in the “Just For You” section of the page.

Nutrition: Observe whether participants can browse and select the first generated “Based on my Labs” meal in Nutrition’s “Just For You” page.

Search:Observe whether participants are able to locate and use the search feature.

TEST EVALUATION:

The usability tests made it clear that the functionality of 2 core features raised major usability issues that need immediate attention:FOUR -WEEK PROGRAM SELECTION and MEAL SELECTION.

User Testing errors, observations and quotes were entered into a Rainbow Spreadsheet, which helps organize findings and create visual patterns.

ITERATE

“You iterate until you get it right from the customers perspective. This is the power of HCD.” -- Olivier Delarue

User interviews revealed significant user errors in the preliminary design of Wellthy’s key features. Based o this feedback, changes were implemented into the design!

DESIGN ITERATIONS:

  1. The first screen of the Exercise feature was removed and integrated into the activity screen, simplifying the design and eliminating additional navigation steps.
  2. The filter feature was turned into a FAB button so users could locate it easier when they wanted it, and "Favorites" are now displayed directly on the screen, reducing the number of clicks it takes for users to navigate around the Exercise feature.
  3. The color contrasts in this current iteration of Wellthy pass the WCAG (AA) Accessibility guidelines.
  4. Added dialog boxes to the My Health "Record Sync" feature will guide the user step- by-step when they are ready to add their health systems to Wellthy.

The design adheres to Principles of Design, Gestalt Laws of Grouping and Material Design guidelines.

PRESENT

Done is better than perfect.

FINDINGS:

The goal was to create a product that granted users access to personalized health and wellness advice on their mobile devices. User interviews revealed that the target audience was not finding an easy-to-use app that offered content relevant to their specific and individual needs. So, Wellthy was designed to be a comprehensive wellness tracking tool allowing users to manage their medical records, conditions, nutrition, and exercise routines in one place.

Although Wellthy was created using a human-centered, iterative design process, it came together with errors. Notably, the scenarios written for user testing proved unclear, complicated, and misleading, resulting in ambiguous results. To prevent similar mistakes in the future, scenarios will be simplified and broken down into more straightforward, more cohesive tasks

Another roadblock was accessibility. The UI was initially designed without WCAG guidelines, resulting in additional time-consuming iterations. In the future, WCAG guidelines will be applied earlier in the design process to save time and resources.

Wellthy has been through many changes, but it’s not perfect. The beauty of Iterative Design is that there is always room to grow, learn, and harness human nature to build better, user-focused designs.
